    How to Use the Staffing Optimizer

    Getting Started

    Use the location dropdown (top right) to switch between entry points (Katz Lobby, Emergency Dept, etc.). The hourly rate field on the Dashboard tab affects all cost calculations. Everything updates automatically when you change settings.

    1. Optimized Schedule

    A pre-built schedule based on actual visitor volume data. Toggle Week A / Week B to see the alternating weekend rotation. The color coding in the grid shows volume levels: green = low volume (1 staff needed), yellow = medium (1-2 staff), red = high (2-3 staff). The Health Score gauge shows how well the current schedule covers demand. Hover any cell for details.

    2. Volume Analysis

    Visual breakdown of when visitors arrive. The heatmap shows average visitors per hour for each day of the week. The Volume vs Staffing chart overlays recommended staff levels on top of visitor counts. Use this to understand peak times and where coverage matters most.

    3. Schedule Builder

    Drag employees from the left panel onto the schedule grid, or click the shift buttons (Morning/Afternoon/Evening) under each employee. Auto-Fill loads the optimized schedule as a starting point. Red-highlighted cells indicate understaffed hours. Save stores your schedule locally. Export CSV downloads it as a spreadsheet. Validation badges at the top flag gaps, overtime, and weekend coverage issues.

    4. Insights & What-If

    Key metrics: FTE utilization, coverage efficiency, gap hours, and cost per visitor. The Recommendations panel highlights staffing issues and opportunities. Use the What-If Simulator sliders to model scenarios: what happens if you add FTEs, visitor volume changes, or you extend coverage hours? Results update in real time.

    Tip: Employee names can be edited directly on the Optimized Schedule tab by clicking on them. Names are saved in your browser. The Print button (bottom of page) generates a clean printout of all visible tabs.
